Founder

My journey to homeopathy and the mission behind Sahaja

My Journey to Homeopathy is unconventional.

Nearly 40 years ago, my romance with homeopathy began—not in a classroom, but through conviction. I started as a physician in conventional medicine, but something deeper called to me. I longed for a more holistic, humane way of healing. That knowing drew me to homeopathy, where I found both a calling and a challenge.

Like Eklavya in the Mahabharata, I learned from many teachers and forged my own path to the roots - mastering the art of unprejudiced observation and the timeless philosophy of healing within.

My first homeopathic teachers were Dr. H. L. Chitkara and Dr. M. L. Sehgal. Then I discovered Jan Scholten's pioneering work, and soon after, Dr. Rajan Sankaran, whose teachings resonated most deeply. I followed his evolution—and with it, the evolution of homeopathy itself. From his circle I learned from Dr. Dinesh Chauhan, Dr. Shekhar Algundgi, and Dr. Mahesh Gandhi, each carrying his legacy in their own way.

What I Observed

We're living in an exciting era for homeopathy. New insights, concepts, and systems are making profound changes in practice. Yet I've watched sincere students get lost chasing methods.

What gets missed is the very foundation Hahnemann emphasized: unprejudiced observation. Without it, every method remains borrowed. With it, any method can be made your own.

Homeopathy is versatile and deeply forgiving. You can aim for the similimum at many levels—but they're not equal. The deeper the similimum, the more holistic the healing - until the vital force can use the organism for what Hahnemann called "the higher purpose of existence."

I sense an urgency to return to the root, to the foundation of unprejudiced observation, regardless of method. And from that urgency, Sahaja Homeopathy was born.

Sahaja Homeopathy: The Mission

To bring clarity to budding homeopaths so they are grounded in unprejudiced observation. Unfortunately, it's assumed students will develop this skill somehow in practice. The reality? Many don't.

They need to know: unprejudiced observation is not a gift you're born with—it's a skill you train. Sahaja makes it systematic. We provide the training that transforms this implicit hope into explicit competency.

In the age of AI, this has never been more possible—or more necessary. AI handles encyclopedic knowledge. You stay free to do what only humans can: truly perceive another's experience.

Sahaja is not another method to chase. It's the foundation to return to—where presence and perception bloom naturally, and confidence in your own observation becomes unshakeable.
